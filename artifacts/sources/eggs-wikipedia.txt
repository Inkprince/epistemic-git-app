Egg yolks and whole eggs contain significant amounts of protein and choline. A medium/large chicken egg provides approximately 70 kilocalories and 6 grams of protein.

Regarding cholesterol and fat, more than half the calories in eggs come from yolk fat. A 50-gram egg contains roughly five grams of fat, with saturated fat comprising 33 percent, monounsaturated fat 38 percent, polyunsaturated fat 20 percent, and cholesterol 4 percent. The egg white consists primarily of water and protein with minimal cholesterol and fat.

There is ongoing debate about whether egg yolks pose health risks. Some research indicates dietary cholesterol raises the total-to-HDL cholesterol ratio negatively, while other studies show that consuming up to one egg daily does not increase heart disease risk in healthy people.

Regarding cardiovascular health, findings are mixed. One systematic review found that higher egg consumption correlated with reduced coronary artery disease risk. However, another analysis found associations with increased all-cause mortality and cardiovascular disease mortality. A 2018 meta-analysis of randomized trials showed eggs increase total and LDL cholesterol compared to no egg consumption. More recent reviews from 2020 concluded that moderate daily egg consumption shows no increased cardiovascular disease risk.
